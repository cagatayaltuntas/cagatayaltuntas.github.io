Harmonic numbers have fascinated mathematicians since the time of Pythagoras. In recent works, generalizations involving hyperharmonic numbers have opened new avenues in studying specific summation identities and combinatorial arrangements.

This article summarizes our recent publication regarding the integration properties of generalized harmonic sequences and their implications for asymptotic bounds.
